Sayso - Identity Document

Why Sayso Exists
Sayso exists because outbound real estate conversations break down in predictable ways during the call. Agents talk to enough people and put in the effort. They make the calls but don't book appointments. In too many conversations, the agent loses direction, misses key questions, or moves to a meeting before the prospect understands why the meeting makes sense. Those moments cannot be fixed later. By the time a call is reviewed or coached, the outcome is already set.

Sayso was built to support agents during the call, when execution lacks and outcomes are still controllable.

The Problem We Are Built Around
Outbound calls are unpredictable by nature. Prospects say things agents do not expect, conversations go off topic, and agents can lose control quickly. When this happens during the call, important questions do not get asked. The conversation loses direction. Agents freeze when a prospect says something emotional or unexpected. Silence gets filled with rambling, and agents talk more than they need to listen. Most coaching does not fix this. Feedback is given well after the moments have passed. Sayso exists to support agents during the call, when outcomes are still controllable.

Why This Problem Matters
This problem is easy to miss because activity looks like progress. Agents stay busy, make calls, and book some appointments, which creates the sense that things are working. But when you look closer, the results do not hold up. A large share of booked appointments do not happen. Conversations feel inconsistent from call to call. Outcomes depend more on how the prospect shows up than on how the agent runs the conversation. Over time, this becomes expensive. Hours are spent on conversations that never turn into real meetings. Calendars fill with appointments that fall apart. Follow up drags on longer than it should. Agents stay busy without building momentum, and leaders struggle to separate effort from effectiveness. The cost is slower growth, inconsistent income, and a constant feeling of starting over.

Book appointments that actually happen

Improve execution without waiting for coaching
